Hobbies

My long-term goal is to work at the intersection of fracture mechanics, materials design, and additive manufacturing, with a focus on improving the reliability of AM-built components and making these processes more viable for real-world use. I am particularly interested in understanding material behavior and failure mechanisms so that design and manufacturing decisions are driven by performance and sustainability. Over time, I hope to contribute through research-oriented roles, either in advanced industrial R&D or through doctoral research, to help enable broader adoption of additive manufacturing technologies.

Outside academics, I actively play competitive cricket and have captained my school U16 team. Being part of a team sport has played a big role in shaping how I work with others, handle pressure, and stay disciplined over long periods of training and competition. Apart from sports, I enjoy watching films in my free time, which helps me unwind and step away from technical work for a bit.
